About this project

This article explores how technical writers can effectively work with unresponsive Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) — one of the most common and frustrating challenges in professional technical writing. The piece focuses on the importance of building positive relationships, navigating communication challenges, and using soft skills to keep documentation projects on track.

The real challenge in TW: Technical accuracy depends on SME input. When SMEs don't respond, documentation stalls. The solution is not to chase harder — it's to make collaboration easy, predictable, and worth their time.

What the article covers

  • Why SMEs go silent — understanding their perspective: competing priorities, unclear asks, fear of being misquoted
  • Setting expectations upfront — how to frame review requests so SMEs know exactly what's needed and how long it will take
  • Professional follow-up strategies — escalation paths that maintain the relationship while keeping the project moving
  • Using tact and diplomacy — how tone and framing affect whether an SME becomes a collaborator or an obstacle
  • Building lasting relationships — turning one-time interactions into ongoing partnerships that make future projects faster

Key skills demonstrated

  • Soft skills in technical writing — the article makes the case that interpersonal skills are as critical as technical proficiency for a TW career
  • Content creation & writing — well-organised, insightful content that combines practical and strategic advice
  • Markdown & web publishing — written in Markdown, published on GitHub Pages
