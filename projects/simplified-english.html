About this project

In this project, I rewrote my original article "Happily Ever After with Unresponsive SMEs" using Simplified Technical English (STE) — a controlled language defined by the ASD-STE100 standard. The revised article maintains the core content and strategies for working with unresponsive Subject Matter Experts, but applies strict linguistic rules to ensure maximum clarity and accessibility for international audiences.

ASD-STE100 in practice: Simplified Technical English is not about "writing simply." It is a disciplined, rule-governed approach: approved words only, sentences under 20 words, active voice as default, one idea per sentence. The constraint is the craft.

What ASD-STE100 requires

  • Controlled vocabulary — only approved words from the STE dictionary; technical terms must be defined in a project glossary
  • Sentence length — procedural sentences maximum 20 words; descriptive sentences maximum 25 words
  • Active voice — passive voice permitted only where the agent is unknown or irrelevant
  • One instruction per sentence — no compound instructions joined with "and"
  • Consistent terminology — the same thing always has the same name; no synonyms

What the project demonstrates

  • Simplified English proficiency — applying STE rules throughout a full article, not just selected sentences
  • Content adaptation — taking existing content and restructuring it for a different audience without losing the core message
  • Technical writing for accessibility — making content usable by readers with varying language proficiency and technical background
  • Markdown & GitHub Pages — structured writing and docs-as-code publishing

Why this skill matters

ASD-STE100 is required for aviation, aerospace, and defence documentation (ASD standards are used by Airbus, Boeing, and NATO documentation groups). It is also increasingly adopted in medical devices, manufacturing, and any domain where documentation serves international, multilingual audiences. A technical writer proficient in STE can reduce translation costs by up to 40% and dramatically reduce translation errors.
